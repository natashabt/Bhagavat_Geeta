WHO IS GOD?
-In Hinduism, "Bhagavan" is a term used to refer to the Supreme Being or the Absolute Truth conceived as a personal god. Bhagavan comes from the Sanskrit word "bhaga," meaning "fortune" or "wealth." Bhagavan refers to the supreme controller, the origin of all beings or the source of everything and comprises six opulences:
  1. 1.beauty
  2. 2.wealth
  3. 3.knowledge
  4. 4.stength
  5. 5.fame
  6. 6.renunciation
From the Bhagavad Gita and other scriptures, we come to know that Lord Krishna, the supreme personality of Godhead, possesses all the opulences in full. He is the supreme controller and the origin of all beings.
